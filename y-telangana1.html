About Mecca Masjid

Mecca Masjid, also spelled Makkah Masjid, is one of the largest mosques in India and a major historical site in Hyderabad. Commissioned by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah in 1614, the mosque gets its name from the bricks used in its construction, which were made from soil brought from Mecca.

The mosque is renowned for its impressive architecture, large prayer hall, and intricate arches. It can accommodate over 10,000 worshippers and is a key religious and cultural landmark in Telangana.
