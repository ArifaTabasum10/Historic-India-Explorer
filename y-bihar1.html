About Bodh Gaya

Bodh Gaya is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites for Buddhists around the world. It is the place where Prince Siddhartha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree and became the Buddha. Located in Bihar, India, it is home to the Mahabodhi Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The Mahabodhi Temple complex includes the revered Bodhi Tree, Vajrasana (the Diamond Throne), and various stupas and monasteries built by Buddhist communities from across the globe. The serene atmosphere, historical depth, and spiritual significance make Bodh Gaya a profound destination for seekers and travelers alike.
