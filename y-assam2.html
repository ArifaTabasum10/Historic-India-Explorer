About Talatal Ghar

Talatal Ghar is a historic military base and palace built during the reign of Ahom ruler Swargadeo Rudra Singha in the early 18th century in Sivasagar, Assam. It is considered one of the largest and most elaborate examples of Ahom architecture.

The structure originally had seven stories, three of which were underground. These subterranean levels were used as escape routes and secret tunnels during wartime. The visible part of the Talatal Ghar still reflects the grandeur of Ahom engineering, made using indigenous materials like bricks and a traditional mortar blend of rice and eggs.
