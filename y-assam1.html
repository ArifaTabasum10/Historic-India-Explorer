About Rang Ghar

Rang Ghar, located in Sivasagar, Assam, is one of the oldest surviving amphitheaters in Asia. It was constructed in the mid-18th century during the reign of Ahom ruler Swargadeo Pramatta Singha. The structure was used as a royal sports pavilion where kings and nobles watched traditional games, including buffalo fights and other cultural performances.

The monument is an architectural marvel with a unique egg-shaped dome and Indo-Ahom design influences. Built with baked bricks and a rice-paste mortar known as 'khar', Rang Ghar represents the glory and sophistication of the Ahom dynasty. It is a protected heritage site under the Archaeological Survey of India.
