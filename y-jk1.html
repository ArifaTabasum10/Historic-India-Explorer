About Amarnath Cave Temple

The Amarnath Cave Temple is one of the most revered shrines in Hinduism, dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is located in the Himalayas at an altitude of about 3,888 meters (12,756 ft) in the Indian union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. The temple is famous for the naturally occurring ice Shiva Lingam that forms during the summer.

Every year, thousands of pilgrims undertake the Amarnath Yatra, a challenging but spiritually significant trek through scenic and rugged terrain. The journey offers not only religious fulfillment but also breathtaking views of the Himalayas.
