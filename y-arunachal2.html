About Tawang Monastery

Tawang Monastery, located in the town of Tawang in Arunachal Pradesh, is the largest monastery in India and the second-largest in the world. It was founded by Merak Lama Lodre Gyatso in 1680 and is an important center of Mahayana Buddhism.

Perched at an elevation of about 10,000 feet, the monastery offers stunning views of the Tawang valley. It houses a massive 8-meter high statue of Lord Buddha, an extensive library of ancient scriptures, and valuable thangkas (Tibetan Buddhist paintings). The monastery is a spiritual, cultural, and architectural treasure, drawing visitors and pilgrims from across the globe.
