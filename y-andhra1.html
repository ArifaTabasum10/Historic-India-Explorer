About Lepakshi Temple

Lepakshi Temple, also known as the Veerabhadra Temple, is a 16th-century marvel built by the Vijayanagara rulers. It is renowned for its intricate carvings, hanging pillar, and exquisite frescoes that adorn the ceilings of its mandapas (halls).

The temple is dedicated to Lord Veerabhadra, an incarnation of Lord Shiva. It features striking architectural elements such as the Nandi bull statue carved from a single granite stone and the famous footprint believed to be of Sita. It is an important example of Vijayanagara architecture and a site of great cultural and historical significance.
